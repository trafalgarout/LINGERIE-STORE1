## Fabric Guide: Choosing Your Sleep Surface

When selecting **premium nightwear brands**, the fabric is the most critical factor.

### 1. Silk (Mulberry Silk)
*   **Best For:** Temperature regulation, anti-aging, luxury feel.
*   **Maintenance:** High. Usually hand-wash or dry clean (unless it's Lunya).
*   **Look:** High shine, fluid drape.

### 2. TENCEL™ / Modal
*   **Best For:** Softness, breathability, eco-conscious shoppers.
*   **Maintenance:** Low. Machine wash cold.
*   **Look:** Matte, jersey-like drape.

### 3. Linen
*   **Best For:** Hot climates, summer, the "lived-in" look.
*   **Maintenance:** Medium. Softens with every wash but wrinkles easily.
*   **Look:** Textured, relaxed.

### 4. Premium Cotton (Pima/Egyptian)
*   **Best For:** Crisp feel, structure, breathability.
*   **Maintenance:** Low. Machine washable.
*   **Look:** Structured, matte.

## FAQ: Premium Nightwear

**Q: Is silk better than cotton for sleep?**
A: For skin benefits and temperature regulation, yes. Silk causes less friction (meaning fewer sleep creases) and doesn't absorb night creams like cotton does.

**Q: Why are designer pajamas so expensive?**
A: It comes down to the raw material. Grade 6A Mulberry silk (the gold standard) is expensive to harvest. Add to that hand-painted prints (which require more screens for printing) and French seams, and the cost rises.

**Q: Can I put sophisticated loungewear in the dryer?**
A: Never. Heat destroys the fibers of silk and modal, causing them to pill or shrink. Always air dry your **premium nightwear brands**.

**Q: What is the difference between satin and silk?**
A: Silk is a fiber; satin is a weave. You can have polyester satin (cheap, sweaty) or silk satin (luxurious, breathable). Always check the label for "100% Silk".
