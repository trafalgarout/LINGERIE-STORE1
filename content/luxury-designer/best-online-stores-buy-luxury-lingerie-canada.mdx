### 1. Customs & Duties
This is the single biggest concern for Canadian luxury shoppers. Here is the reality:
*   **Under CAD $20:** No duty or tax (this threshold is too low for luxury lingerie).
*   **Over CAD $20:** Subject to up to 18% customs duty plus provincial sales tax (GST/HST).
*   **The Workaround:** Many international retailers now offer "Delivered Duty Paid" (DDP) shipping, where all taxes and duties are collected at checkout—no surprise charges on delivery.

### 2. Canadian vs. US Sizing
Canadian sizing follows the US system (32A, 34B, 36C, etc.), which simplifies shopping from American retailers. However, European brands use different sizing (French, Italian, UK), so always check brand-specific conversion charts.

### 3. The Bilingual Advantage
Canada's Francophone population has a natural affinity for French lingerie brands. Brands like **Aubade**, **Simone Pérèle**, and **Chantelle** have a built-in audience in Quebec, and several Canadian retailers specifically curate French collections.

## Shopping Tips for Canadian Buyers

### 1. Use the CAD Advantage
When the Canadian dollar is strong against the Euro or British Pound, buying directly from European brands can save 20-30% compared to Canadian retail prices. Check the exchange rate before purchasing from European **high-end lingerie websites**.

### 2. Cross-Border Shopping (Wisely)
If you live near the US border, shipping to a US address (via a parcel forwarding service or a "PO Box" just across the border) can save on duties for orders over $20. Services like **Kinek** and **CrossBorderPickups** facilitate this.

Several international retailers periodically offer "duty-free" promotions to Canadian customers, absorbing the import costs. NET-A-PORTER and Matches Fashion have run these promotions during Black Friday and holiday seasons.

## FAQ: Buying Luxury Lingerie in Canada

**Q: Why is luxury lingerie more expensive in Canada?**
A: A combination of import duties (up to 18% on textile goods), GST/HST (5-15% depending on province), and the CAD/USD exchange rate. Buying from Canadian-based retailers like SSENSE or Simons eliminates the duty component.

**Q: Can I avoid customs duties?**
A: Legally, duties apply to most imported goods over $20 CAD. However, many retailers now offer DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) shipping, where duties are pre-calculated and included in the checkout price. SSENSE avoids duties entirely by shipping domestically from Montreal.

**Q: What is the best store for French lingerie in Canada?**
A: **Simons** has the strongest French lingerie curation, reflecting Quebec's cultural ties. **Holt Renfrew** also carries major French brands. For the widest online selection, **Journelle** ships to Canada with reasonable rates.

**Q: Are there any luxury lingerie subscription services in Canada?**
A: The subscription lingerie market is still emerging in Canada. However, **Nordstrom's "Trunk Club"** allows you to receive a curated box of lingerie to try at home, keeping only what you want.
