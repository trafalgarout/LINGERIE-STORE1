## Fabric Guide for Brides

When choosing **luxury bridal underwear**, the fabric dictates the feel and the fit.

### 1. Silk Satin
*   **Pros:** Smooth, cool, and incredibly shiny. Glides under dresses.
*   **Cons:** No stretch. Sizing must be precise.

### 2. Chantilly Lace
*   **Pros:** The most delicate and romantic lace. Very lightweight.
*   **Cons:** Fragile. Jewelry can snag it easily.

### 3. Tulle (Bobbinet)
*   **Pros:** Sheer and sexy. Often used as a backing for embroidery.
*   **Cons:** Can be scratchy if cheap. Look for "silk tulle" for softness.

### 4. Guipure Lace
*   **Pros:** Distinctive, heavy, 3D texture.
*   **Cons:** Can show through thin wedding dress fabrics (like crepe or silk bias).

## How to Match Lingerie to Your Dress

Your **high-end wedding lingerie** choice should be dictated by your gown's silhouette.

### The Ballgown
You have the most freedom here. A full corset, a basque, or a full set of **luxury bridal lingerie** with garters will all fit effortlessly underneath.

### The Mermaid / Trumpet
You need seamlessness. Look for high-waisted shaping briefs from brands like **Commando** or flat-lace thongs from **Hanky Panky** (their bridal line). Save the textured **I.D. Sarrieri** set for the wedding night.

### The Slip Dress / Bias Cut
This is the trickiest. You likely need a "stick-on" solution for the bust (like **NuBra**) and a seamless silk thong. Or, embrace the 1930s vibe and wear a bias-cut silk slip from **Carine Gilson** underneath.

## The Honeymoon: Building a Trousseau
The "trousseau" used to be a bundle of linens a bride brought to her marriage. Today, it's a bundle of **premium honeymoon lingerie**.
*   **Day 1-3:** Pack 3-4 "special" sets. Think colors—blush pink, midnight blue, or emerald green. You are done with the white!
*   **Day 4-7:** Pack luxury loungewear. Silk camisoles and tap shorts are perfect for breakfast on the balcony.

## FAQ: Bridal Lingerie

**Q: When should I buy my wedding lingerie?**
A: Buy your foundation garments *before* your first dress fitting. Your seamstress needs to tailor the dress over exactly what you will be wearing. Buy your **elegant wedding night lingerie** about 1-2 months before the wedding.

**Q: Should I wear a corset under my dress?**
A: Only if your dress doesn't have one built-in. Doubling up can be bulky and uncomfortable.

**Q: What is the tradition of the garter?**
A: Historically, it was a symbol of luck. Today, many brides skip the "toss" but wear a **luxury bridal lingerie** garter as a private keepsake. Brands like **Florange** make exquisite keepsake garters.

**Q: How do I transport my lingerie?**
A: Use a dedicated lingerie travel bag. We recommend silk or cotton bags to let the fabric breathe, rather than plastic.
