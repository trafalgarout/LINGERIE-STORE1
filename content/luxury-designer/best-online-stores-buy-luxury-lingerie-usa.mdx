## Shopping Tips: How to Buy Smart

### 1. Know Your Size in Multiple Systems
US, UK, French, and Italian sizing all differ. Before purchasing from a **high-end lingerie website**, convert your size using the brand's specific chart—not a generic converter.

### 2. Read the Fabric Composition
A $200 bra should not be 100% polyester. Check the composition label in the product details. Look for:
*   **Silk** (at least in the lining)
*   **Leavers Lace** or **Guipure Lace** (not "polyester lace")
*   **Modal** or **Supima Cotton** for everyday pieces

### 3. Check the Return Window
**Premium lingerie USA shipping** often takes 3-5 business days. If the return window is only 14 days from *shipping date*, you may have less than a week to decide. Prefer stores with 30-day return windows.

### 4. Sign Up for Newsletters
Luxury lingerie brands rarely do public sales, but they send exclusive discounts (10-20% off) to email subscribers. Sign up for:
*   **Journelle** (frequent subscriber-only events)
*   **La Perla** (archive access and private sales)
*   **SSENSE** (early access to seasonal sales)

## FAQ: Buying Luxury Lingerie Online

**Q: Is it safe to buy lingerie online without trying it on?**
A: Yes, if you choose the right store. Prioritize retailers with free returns and detailed size guides. Brands like Journelle and Net-a-Porter make the process virtually risk-free.

**Q: How do I avoid counterfeits?**
A: Only buy from authorized retailers or the brand's official website. Avoid third-party marketplaces unless the seller is verified. Check the tags—authentic luxury lingerie will have detailed composition labels, country of origin, and care instructions in multiple languages.

**Q: Can I buy luxury lingerie as a gift?**
A: Absolutely. Net-a-Porter and Agent Provocateur offer gift wrapping and personalized notes. Journelle has a specific "gifting" section with sizing tips for partners.

**Q: What about customs/duties on international brands?**
A: Most **best online lingerie stores USA** handle customs clearance for you. Net-a-Porter shows duties at checkout, so there are no surprise charges on delivery. La Perla ships from US warehouses, eliminating duties entirely.
